HARLEQUIN

2025 | 1-week personal project

3D Character Adaptation for Wild Rift

Dark Harlequin is a one-week personal project focused on adapting an existing 2D concept into a stylized, game-ready 3D character inspired by the art direction of Wild Rift.
The goal was to test my speed, artistic decisions, and technical workflow under a short production time.

Final In-Game Mockup

Final render imagined in an in-game context. The integration and final visual were created in Photoshop to simulate how the character could appear inside Wild Rift. This image focuses on silhouette readability, color clarity, and overall visual impact in a gameplay environment.

From 2D Concept to 3D
Art Direction Adaptation

Side-by-side comparison between the original 2D concept by Marie Magny, my 3D adaptation, and an existing Wild Rift champion used as an art direction reference. This comparison highlights the artistic choices made to adapt proportions, shapes, materials, and color palette to fit Wild Rift's stylized visual language.

Turntable

Turntable showcasing the full 360Â° view of the character with final textures created in Substance 3D Painter. The texturing process focused on stylized materials, hand-painted details, and controlled roughness values to ensure clarity and consistency with the Wild Rift visual style.

Production Pipeline

Overview of the main production stages: high poly sculpting in ZBrush, quick retopology using ZBrush and Blender, low poly wireframe, and texturing in Substance Painter. This pipeline reflects a fast and efficient workflow adapted to a short production timeframe.

High Poly Sculpt

Front and back views of the high poly sculpt created in ZBrush. The sculpt was intentionally kept clean, with most ornamental patterns and secondary surface details omitted at this stage. This choice was made to avoid unnecessary geometry and to keep the future low poly asset optimized for real-time use. These details were later introduced during the texturing phase using height information in Substance 3D Painter, allowing for visual depth without increasing polygon count.

Post-Mortem

This project was a valuable exercise in speed and decision-making. Working within a one-week timeframe helped me improve my ability to prioritize visual impact and art direction adaptation over excessive detailing.
However, the retopology phase was rushed, resulting in topology that is not as optimized as it should be for a fully game-ready character.
In future projects, I would dedicate more time to cleaner edge flow, better polygon distribution, and animation-ready topology. This experience reinforced the importance of balancing production speed with technical quality.

Thank you for following along with this project!
